# Glossary of Customs and Trade Terms

**Comprehensive Dictionary for JUL System Users**

---

## A

**AEO (Authorized Economic Operator)**  
A trusted trader certified by customs for compliance and security standards. Benefits include simplified procedures, reduced inspections, and priority treatment.

**AGT (Administração Geral Tributária)**  
Angola Customs Authority responsible for customs administration, revenue collection, and border control.

**AOA (Angolan Kwanza)**  
Official currency of Angola (ISO 4217 code: AOA).

**ASYCUDA (Automated System for Customs Data)**  
UNCTAD-developed customs management system used by Angola. Handles declaration processing, duty calculation, and customs clearance.

**Airway Bill (AWB)**  
Transport document for air cargo, serving as evidence of the contract of carriage and receipt of goods.

---

## B

**Bill of Lading (B/L)**  
Maritime transport document issued by a carrier, acknowledging receipt of cargo for shipment.

**Bonded Warehouse**  
Secure facility where goods can be stored without payment of import duties and taxes, under customs supervision.

**Border Clearance**  
Process of obtaining customs authorization to import or export goods across international borders.

**Broker's License**  
Authorization to act as a customs broker, facilitating customs clearance on behalf of traders.

---

## C

**C&F (Cost and Freight)**  
Incoterm where the seller pays cost and freight to the destination port, but risk transfers to buyer when goods are loaded.

**CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight)**  
Incoterm where the seller pays all costs including insurance to the destination port.

**CNCA (Certificado Nacional de Capacidade Aduaneira)**  
National Customs Capacity Certificate required for companies engaged in import/export activities in Angola.

**Certificate of Origin**  
Document certifying the country where goods were manufactured or produced, required for preferential tariff treatment.

**Clearance Certificate**  
Official document issued by customs after payment of duties, authorizing cargo release.

**Classification**  
Process of determining the correct HS code for goods to calculate appropriate duties and taxes.

**Consignee**  
Person or company to whom goods are shipped and who receives the cargo at destination.

**Consignor**  
Person or company who sends or ships goods (exporter/shipper).

**Container**  
Standardized metal box for transporting goods (20ft, 40ft, etc.), identified by ISO 6346 format.

**CUSDEC**  
UN/EDIFACT Customs Declaration message sent to ASYCUDA system.

**CUSRES**  
UN/EDIFACT Customs Response message received from ASYCUDA with assessment results.

**Customs Broker**  
Licensed professional who prepares and submits customs documentation on behalf of importers/exporters.

**Customs Declaration**  
Official statement describing goods being imported or exported, required for customs clearance.

**Customs Duty**  
Tax imposed on imported goods, calculated based on CIF value and applicable tariff rate.

**Customs Value**  
Value of goods for customs purposes, typically CIF value (Cost, Insurance, Freight).

---

## D

**Declarant**  
Person or entity making the customs declaration (may be importer, exporter, or their authorized agent).

**DU (Declaração Única)**  
Single Declaration form used in Angola for customs clearance of imports and exports.

**Duty**  
Tax levied on imported or exported goods by customs authorities.

**Duty Assessment**  
Calculation of import duties, taxes, and fees payable on a customs declaration.

**Duty Drawback**  
Refund of duties paid on imported goods that are subsequently exported or used in export production.

---

## E

**EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)**  
Computer-to-computer exchange of business documents in a standard electronic format.

**EDIFACT (Electronic Data Interchange For Administration, Commerce and Transport)**  
UN standard for EDI messages, used in customs for CUSDEC and CUSRES messages.

**EXW (Ex Works)**  
Incoterm where the seller makes goods available at their premises; buyer bears all costs and risks from that point.

**Excise Duty**  
Special tax imposed on specific goods like alcohol, tobacco, and petroleum products.

**Export Declaration**  
Customs document required when goods leave the country's customs territory.

---

## F

**FCL (Full Container Load)**  
Shipping mode where one shipper's cargo fills an entire container.

**FOB (Free on Board)**  
Incoterm where the seller delivers goods on board the vessel; buyer pays freight and insurance.

**Freight Forwarder**  
Company that organizes shipments and related services on behalf of shippers.

**Free Trade Zone**  
Designated area where goods may be imported, stored, and processed without payment of duties.

---

## G

**GRI (General Rules for Interpretation)**  
Six rules established by WCO for classifying goods under the Harmonized System.

**Gross Weight**  
Total weight of goods including packaging materials.

---

## H

**HS Code (Harmonized System Code)**  
6-digit international commodity classification code developed by WCO, extended to 8-10 digits nationally.

**HS 2017**  
Harmonized System nomenclature version effective from 2017.

**HS 2022**  
Latest Harmonized System nomenclature version with updates effective from 2022.

---

## I

**IAM (Identity and Access Management)**  
System for managing user identities, authentication, and authorization (Keycloak in JUL).

**Import Declaration**  
Customs document submitted when bringing goods into the country.

**Import Duty**  
Tax imposed on imported goods, calculated as percentage of customs value.

**Import License**  
Authorization required to import certain goods (restricted or controlled items).

**Incoterms (International Commercial Terms)**  
Standard trade terms defining responsibilities of buyers and sellers in international transactions.

**Inspection**  
Physical examination of goods by customs to verify declaration accuracy.

---

## J

**JUL (Janela Única Logística)**  
Angola National Logistics Single Window System for trade facilitation.

---

## K

**Keycloak**  
Open-source identity and access management system used in JUL for authentication and authorization.

---

## L

**LCL (Less than Container Load)**  
Shipping mode where multiple shippers' cargo is consolidated in one container.

**LPCO (Licenses, Permits, Certificates, and Other documents)**  
Regulatory documents required by government agencies for certain goods.

---

## M

**Manifest**  
List of all cargo on a vessel, aircraft, or vehicle, submitted to customs before arrival.

**MFN (Most Favored Nation)**  
Standard tariff rate applied to imports from WTO member countries without preferential agreements.

**MinIO**  
S3-compatible object storage system used in JUL for document management.

---

## N

**Net Weight**  
Weight of goods excluding packaging materials.

**NIF (Número de Identificação Fiscal)**  
Tax Identification Number in Angola, required for all businesses.

**Non-Tariff Barrier**  
Trade restriction other than customs duties (quotas, licenses, standards, etc.).

---

## O

**OGA (Other Government Agencies)**  
Government departments beyond customs that regulate specific goods (health, agriculture, standards, etc.).

**Origin**  
Country where goods were produced or manufactured, relevant for preferential tariffs.

**Origin Certificate**  
Document certifying the country of origin, required for preferential duty rates.

---

## P

**Packing List**  
Detailed list of goods in a shipment, including quantities, weights, and dimensions.

**Port of Entry**  
Location where goods officially enter a country's customs territory.

**Port of Loading**  
Location where goods are loaded onto transport vessel.

**Post-Clearance Audit**  
Customs verification of declarations after cargo release, conducted within 3 years.

**Preferential Tariff**  
Reduced duty rate for goods from countries with trade agreements (SADC, EU, etc.).

**Prohibited Goods**  
Items banned from import or export by law.

**Pro Forma Invoice**  
Preliminary invoice provided before shipment, used for customs valuation.

---

## Q

**Quota**  
Quantitative limit on imports or exports of specific goods during a period.

**QR Code**  
Quick Response barcode used on clearance certificates for verification.

---

## R

**Release Order**  
Authorization for terminal to release cargo after customs clearance.

**Restricted Goods**  
Items requiring special permits or licenses for import/export.

**Risk Assessment**  
Automated evaluation of declaration to determine inspection level (Green/Yellow/Orange/Red channel).

---

## S

**SADC (Southern African Development Community)**  
Regional organization providing preferential trade arrangements for member states including Angola.

**Revised Kyoto Convention (RKC)**  
International Convention on the Simplification and Harmonization of Customs Procedures, establishing principles for modern customs operations including transparency, standardization, risk management, and partnership with trade.

**SAFE Framework**  
WCO Framework of Standards to Secure and Facilitate Global Trade, establishing customs-to-customs network arrangements and customs-to-business partnerships (AEO programs).

**SOLAS (Safety of Life at Sea)**  
International Maritime Organization convention establishing minimum safety standards for construction, equipment, and operation of ships, including VGM requirements for container weights.

**Seal Number**  
Unique identifier on container seals, verified during customs inspection.

**Single Window**  
Electronic platform allowing traders to submit all import/export documentation through one system.

**SINTECE (Sistema Nacional de Troca Electrónica de Dados)**  
Angola National Electronic Data Exchange System (Single Window).

**Statistical Value**  
FOB value plus freight and insurance, used for trade statistics.

**Supplementary Units**  
Additional quantity measure required for certain HS codes (liters, pieces, pairs, etc.).

**TFA (Trade Facilitation Agreement)**  
WTO agreement aimed at expediting the movement, release, and clearance of goods, establishing measures for effective cooperation between customs and other authorities on trade facilitation and customs compliance.

**Time Release Study (TRS)**  
WCO tool for measuring the time required for release of goods, identifying bottlenecks and priorities for trade facilitation measures.

**UN/CEFACT**  
United Nations Centre for Trade Facilitation and Electronic Business, developing standards and recommendations for trade facilitation and electronic business.

**UNTDED (UN Trade Data Elements Directory)**  
Directory of standardized trade data elements, providing a common reference for data required in international trade.

**VGM (Verified Gross Mass)**  
Safety measure under SOLAS requiring shippers to verify and document the total gross mass of packed containers before loading onto vessels.

**Incoterms® 2020**  
International Commercial Terms published by ICC, defining responsibilities of buyers and sellers in international transactions (e.g., FOB, CIF, EXW, DDP).

**GTIN (Global Trade Item Number)**  
GS1 identification number used to uniquely identify products in the supply chain, encoded in barcodes.

**EPCIS (Electronic Product Code Information Services)**  
GS1 standard for sharing supply chain event data, enabling track and trace of goods through the supply chain.

**IMDG Code**  
International Maritime Dangerous Goods Code, IMO regulations for safe transport of dangerous goods by sea.

**e-AWB (Electronic Air Waybill)**  
IATA standard for paperless air cargo documentation, replacing paper air waybills with electronic versions.

**AEO (Authorized Economic Operator)**  
WCO/EU trusted trader program certifying businesses meeting supply chain security and customs compliance standards, qualifying for trade facilitation benefits.

**CBM (Coordinated Border Management)**  
WCO framework for cooperation and coordination among border control agencies to improve efficiency and effectiveness of border management.

---

## T

**Tariff**  
Schedule of duties imposed on imported or exported goods.

**Tariff Classification**  
Process of determining correct HS code for goods.

**TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit)**  
Standard measure of container capacity (one 20-foot container = 1 TEU).

**Transit Declaration**  
Customs document for goods passing through a country to another destination.

**Transhipment**  
Transfer of cargo from one vessel to another during transport to final destination.

---

## U

**UN/CEFACT (United Nations Centre for Trade Facilitation and Electronic Business)**  
UN body developing standards for trade facilitation including EDIFACT.

**UN/EDIFACT**  
United Nations Electronic Data Interchange for Administration, Commerce and Transport.

**UN/LOCODE (United Nations Code for Trade and Transport Locations)**  
Standardized codes for ports, airports, and inland locations worldwide.

**Under-Invoicing**  
Fraudulent practice of declaring lower value than actual to reduce duty payments.

---

## V

**Valuation**  
Process of determining customs value of imported goods for duty calculation.

**VAT (Value Added Tax)**  
Consumption tax applied to dutiable value (CIF + customs duty).

**VGM (Verified Gross Mass)**  
Certified total weight of packed container, required before loading on vessel.

---

## W

**WAT (West Africa Time)**  
Time zone used in Angola (UTC+1).

**WCO (World Customs Organization)**  
Intergovernmental organization establishing international customs standards and best practices.

**WCO Data Model**  
Standardized data structure for customs declarations developed by WCO (current version: 3.10).

**Weight Declaration**  
Statement of net and gross weight of goods, required in customs declaration.

---

## X

**X-ray Inspection**  
Non-intrusive cargo scanning technology used for customs examination.
